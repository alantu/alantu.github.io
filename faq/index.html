Frequently Asked Questions

What's Alantu?

The first crowd-processing mobile solution based on a gamified community, is an app that gives you a way to contribute to research and development in different fields (such as astronomy, climate, physics, medicine and others). Many problems require a vast amounts of computing power and Alantu enables you to include your Android device as part of a distributed computing system when you are not actively using your Android device.

How do I get started?

Search for Alantu in the Google Play Store, and install it. When it launches, you can be a Hunter or a Sleeper. A Hunter administrate a grid of Sleepers. Register for an account to get started. As a Hunter, you´ll be able to select which project your grid wants to contribute.

Processing would only happen (the app only runs) when the phone is connected to Wi-Fi and when plugged in to an external power source (e.g., wireless chargers, wall socket and personal computers).

What projects can I support?

With Alantu, players can help many researchers in different fields. You can help to explore the farthest reaches of universe and search for intelligent life or you can help crunch the extensive climate change data or, if you want, you can help to find improves to treatments of cancer, AIDS and Alzheimer's

Alantu already provides access to a selection of volunteer computing projects thanks to BOINC (Open Community Platform).

What are the technical requirements?

If your Android device is compatible and meets the following requirements, you can install Alantu:

  • Android 4.3 or above.
  • 1.5GHz Dual Core, or 1GHz Quad core.
  • 1GB RAM

Which devices are compatible with Alantu?

Alantu is currently supporting Samsung Galaxy Note 2, Samsung Galaxy Note 3, Samsung Galaxy S3, Samsung Galaxy S4, Samsung Galaxy S4 Active, Samsung Galaxy S4 mini, Samsung Galaxy S5,HTC One (M8), HTC One, HTC One max, HTC One mini, HTC One mini 2, HTC Desire 816, HTC Desire 610, HTC Butterfly, HTC Butterfly s, Google Nexus 4, Google Nexus 5, Motorola Moto G, Motorola Moto X, LG G2.

Will my device get overheated?

The default maximum battery temperature is 40°C. If the device temperature reaches 40°C, Alantu will stop running.

Will my battery life be affected?

Alantu will only be actively contributing when your device is plugged into an external power source and your battery is full (100%) charge (default setting). Your device’s battery life will not be significantly affected. Furthermore, your recharging time will not be significantly extended.

Will participating consume minutes in my mobile data plan?

No. Alantu will only be transferring data when your device is connected to a Wi-Fi network.

Is Alantu safe to use?

The email and password transmission between the project server and your phone is established through HTTPS (Hypertext Transfer Protocol Secure).

The app and the programs it runs only uses the CPU to run calculations for the supported projects and will not access any other data on your device.

Will it affect my phone’s performance?

You can change the amount of memory used by Alantu if you feel that your phone’s system performance is being affected (the default setting is 10% if your device has1 GB RAM, and 25% for anything above 1GB RAM).

Will Alantu work if my device doesn’t have a battery?

Alantu only works on Android devices with batteries.

How do i know how much power I have contributed?

The computations you have contributed to your selected project are measured by means of credits and are shown under the Projects page after it’s being verified by the project server. To learn more, please visit http://boinc.berkeley.edu/wiki/Computation_credit.

What if I want to uninstall Alantu?

You can uninstall the app any time using the standard app uninstallation procedure on your Android device.
